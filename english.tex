\section{Master thesis}
\cvline{title}{\emph{On the mean curvature of Langrangian Surfaces}}
\cvline{supervisors}{Prof. Dr. Matthias Schwarz}
\cvline{description}{\small Surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^3$ are well studied. Because the codimension is one, the first and the second fundamental form are easy to handle. If the surface is an embedding in $\mathbb{R}^4 \cong \mathbb{C}^2$, the codimenstion is two, and the Gauss map and the second fundamental form are more difficult. It was the intention to examine surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^4$, which have a special property - the property of being Lagrangian. The aim was to show --  using methods of symplectic geometry -- that the condition to be Lagrangian makes it possible to write the mean curvature in much simpler terms than usual and in a transferred sense we can treat them like surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^3$.}
